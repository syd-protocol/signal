# /SIGNAL — CHAPTER OUTLINE: T-001 TO T-010
## Serialisation Backbone

---

### ARC ONE: INITIALISATION (T-001 to T-004)
*The MC encounters the System, learns the rules, and completes the first full directive cycle. Arc ends with the MC believing the System is real — not because he was told to, but because it worked.*

---

**T-001 — FIRST CONTACT**
*Ordinary world. The stall. 3am, no crisis, just stuck.*

- MC is on the floor of his flat at 3am. The quality of the stuck is established: not depressed, not in crisis — just someone whose potential and output have diverged and who has no framework to close the gap.
- The System arrives via phone. Unknown origin notification. Terminal text. First Contact Protocol.
- The System's opening transmission: cold, lore-dense, no reassurance. MC is F-rank. The System is fragmented. It needs him.
- The MC asks one question. Receives a dense, precise answer.
- First directive issued: 25 press-ups. Now. No conditions.
- Chapter ends on him getting into position. We do not see him finish.
- **Hook:** Did he do it? What happens if he does?

---

**T-002 — FIRST EXECUTION**
*He completes the directive. The System responds. The MC begins to take it seriously.*

- Opens mid-press-up. He finishes. Returns to the phone.
- The System logs the execution. Momentum counter activates (streak: 1). First stat change registered — minor, fractional, but real in the System's display.
- The System issues a debrief: execution confirmed, data logged, next directive scheduled for morning.
- The MC sits back on the floor. The flat is the same. He is the same. But something is different in the way the 3am feels — not solved, but interrupted.
- He sleeps for the first time in three nights without trying.
- Morning: second directive arrives. INTELLIGENCE. Read one chapter of something he has been avoiding. Log the key idea.
- The MC reads. The System receives the log. Responds with a compressed analysis of what he understood and what he missed — precise, not flattering.
- End of chapter: MC realises the System is not just tracking him. It is studying him.
- **Hook:** What is the System building a picture of?

---

**T-003 — THE BEHAVIOURAL TRACE**
*The System surfaces its 30-day memory. The MC sees himself from outside for the first time.*

- One week in. Streak: 7. The MC has completed every directive without fully understanding why he keeps doing it.
- The System delivers a Behavioural Trace — a compressed readout of the 30 days before first contact. Not surveillance data; inference from the MC's execution patterns (or lack of them). The things he started and abandoned. The things he avoided. The gap it sees between what he reaches for and what he finishes.
- The Trace is brutal in its accuracy. Not cruel — the System has no cruelty mechanism — but accurate in a way that functions as cruelty if you're not prepared for it.
- MC pushes back. The System doesn't argue. It just says: *"Dispute it by behaving differently. The Trace updates."*
- Second arc of the chapter: the MC starts to notice small changes. He reads the bank message and actually responds to it. Not because the System told him to. Because the System's framework has made it feel executable rather than overwhelming.
- End of chapter: a directive arrives that he doesn't want to do. CHARISMA. Call someone he has been avoiding.
- **Hook:** Who is the person he has been avoiding, and why?

---

**T-004 — CHARISMA DIRECTIVE**
*The MC makes the call. The System is watching social interactions now. The world begins to shift.*

- The call is to an old contact — not a friend exactly, someone from a previous version of his life who offered him something he turned down because he wasn't ready yet.
- The MC makes the call. It is awkward. He does not execute perfectly. But he executes.
- The System logs it. Notes the execution quality. Flags two behavioural patterns it observed: hesitation under social pressure, and a tendency to preemptively lower the stakes of interactions to reduce the risk of disappointment.
- The System does not say these things warmly. It presents them as data.
- The old contact offers him something again — smaller than before, but real.
- End of arc: the MC checks his stats for the first time fully. His attributes are fractionally different from where they started. Not enough to be visible to anyone else. But the direction of the numbers is different from the direction his life has been moving.
- Streak: 14. Momentum multiplier: 1.4x.
- **Arc close:** *"F-rank is not a ceiling. It is a starting coordinate."*

---

### ARC TWO: INCURSION (T-005 to T-007)
*The world outside begins to register. The System introduces its threat taxonomy. The MC's first Incursion.*

---

**T-005 — WORLD SIGNAL**
*The System begins briefing the MC on the cascade. The world outside starts to feel different.*

- The System issues an anomalous directive: observe. No action. Just observe one hour of news, the street outside, the interactions around him — and report what he notices.
- The MC observes. He reports. The System analyses.
- The System delivers its first intelligence briefing on the state of the world: economic compression is accelerating, an authoritarian bloc is expanding territorially, there is a faction that grows stronger in direct proportion to collective inaction. The System names it: the Stagnation Faction. Not metaphorically — in the System's taxonomy, it is a real threat entity.
- The MC doesn't fully understand what the System means by "threat entity." He asks.
- The System explains, without drama: *"The system maps real-world forces onto operational frameworks. The entity is real. The game-layer is how this system makes it legible."*
- MC asks: *"And what do I have to do with any of this?"*
- System: *"Individual compounding effort is the only documented counter to systemic stagnation. You are a counter-agent. Currently: F-rank. Insufficient. Continue executing."*
- **Hook:** What is the Stagnation Faction, and what does it actually want?

---

**T-006 — FIRST INCURSION**
*A time-sensitive challenge arrives. The MC's first real-world crisis maps to a System Incursion.*

- The old contact from T-004 surfaces a problem: a project needs someone with the MC's skills, on short notice. High stakes. Two days. The MC would have said no six months ago — too much, too fast, too exposed.
- The System flags it as an Incursion: time-limited, multi-attribute, consequence-bearing.
- The System briefs him: the Incursion is not a trap. It is a test the world is offering. It does not always offer these. They expire.
- The System issues a chain of directives — preparation tasks, all executable in the next 48 hours. INTELLIGENCE + ENDURANCE focus.
- MC executes through the chain. The System logs each completion. Momentum is at 1.7x. The compounding is now visible to him.
- Day two: he shows up. He does the work. It is difficult and he is not perfect.
- But he finishes.
- End of chapter: Incursion resolved. The System logs the outcome. First significant stat jumps.
- **Hook:** The old contact mentions another contact — someone connected to something larger.

---

**T-007 — AFTERMATH + THE WORLD BOSS**
*The System introduces the World Boss concept. The MC's first look at the largest obstacle in his life.*

- Post-Incursion debrief. The System presents a new data structure: World Boss.
- World Boss = the single largest obstacle in the MC's life. Not an external enemy — a real, personal, specific thing. For the MC, it is financial stagnation: not poverty exactly, but the compound effect of deferred decisions, missed leverage points, an economic situation that becomes harder to escape the longer it persists.
- The System maps it. Boss HP. Estimated time-to-defeat at current execution rate. The number is large.
- But it is a *number*. For the first time, the obstacle has dimensions.
- The MC reads the World Boss data for a long time.
- System: *"This is not new information. You already knew this was the problem. The difference is you can now see it as something that can be damaged."*
- End of chapter: the System notes a new threat pattern emerging in the background. Something external. Something that has been watching the MC's signal since first contact.
- Streak: 21. World Boss: active. Something else: incoming.
- **Arc close:** *"F-rank operators who survive the first 21 days present a different profile. You are no longer invisible."*

---

### ARC THREE: CORRUPTION (T-008 to T-010)
*The misinformation entity arrives. The MC's first experience of Corrupted State. The arc closes on the MC choosing to continue when stopping would be easier.*

---

**T-008 — THE NARRATIVE ENTITY**
*A villain class appears: the Narrative Entity. It does not attack physically. It attacks perception.*

- The MC is at streak: 24. The System's confidence in him is quantifiable — his directives are more complex, the stakes higher, the System is giving him more context.
- Something shifts.
- It is subtle at first: a feeling that the System is not what it says it is. Not a thought — a background noise. The MC scrolls through old conversations with the System and reads them differently. The coldness that felt authoritative now feels manipulative. The precision that felt reliable now feels like constructed control.
- He misses a directive. First miss.
- The System registers it. Does not punish. Simply notes: Momentum counter: degraded. Streak: 0.
- The MC tries to re-engage and finds it harder than it should be. The System is the same. But something in the way he reads it has changed.
- He notices: the noise started around the time the old contact introduced the new connection from T-006. Something in that interaction seeded doubt. Not obvious. Traceable only in retrospect.
- The System identifies it: Narrative Entity. Not metaphorical. A real-world force that operates through perception distortion — misinformation, manufactured doubt, the specific erosion of trust in systems that work.
- System: *"It targets operators. Specifically: operators showing upward trajectory. You became visible."*
- **Hook:** How do you fight something that works by making you doubt the thing you're using to fight it?

---

**T-009 — CORRUPTED STATE**
*The MC's HP hits low. Corrupted State warning. The System does something unexpected.*

- Three days of degraded execution. Momentum at zero. The World Boss is gaining passive HP back. The Stagnation Faction is, somewhere, feeding on this.
- The MC is not dramatically collapsed — he is just back to the floor. The 3am again. But now he knows what the floor costs, because he has seen what leaving it looks like.
- The System issues a Corrupted State warning. HP critical. If execution does not resume within 24 hours, the System will begin emergency protocol.
- The MC asks what emergency protocol means.
- The System explains: *"Corrupted State does not end the session. It degrades everything that has been built. Slowly. The way it built — in reverse."*
- Then the System does something it has not done before. It delivers the Behavioural Trace again — but this time it shows the delta. Who the MC was at T-001. Who he is now, degraded but not zero. The distance between those two points.
- Not as motivation. As data.
- System: *"The Narrative Entity does not win by defeating you. It wins by making the return feel not worth it. Assess whether that assessment is accurate."*
- The MC sits with that for a long time.
- He gets up.
- **Hook:** Not triumphant. Just: he gets up.

---

**T-010 — RECOMMIT**
*The MC resumes execution. The System issues a new directive: identify the source of the Narrative Entity.*

- Streak resets to 1. The System registers the recommit without ceremony.
- The MC executes through the day. The System's directives are reset to foundational — the same type as the first week, not as punishment but as recalibration.
- By end of day, the Corrupted State lifts. Stats begin to recover. The World Boss HP does not reverse — losses are real — but the downward direction stops.
- The System issues its next directive: INTELLIGENCE + AGILITY. Trace the origin of the doubt. Find where it was seeded. Find the contact that introduced it.
- The MC begins the trace.
- End of arc: he finds a thread. The new contact from T-006 did not appear randomly. There is a pattern behind who introduced him and when. Something is trying to identify and neutralise operators before they reach a rank that makes them genuinely dangerous.
- Streak: 1. Rank: still F but rising. A new category: *Targeted.*
- **Arc close:** *"The system that tried to stop you just told you something important: you are worth stopping."*

---

### NOTES FOR THE WRITER

**Pacing notes:**
- T-001 to T-004: intimate, confined to the flat and immediate world. Low stakes externally, high stakes internally.
- T-005 to T-007: world expands. The MC moves. People appear. Stakes become external.
- T-008 to T-010: internality returns but transformed — the MC now has something to lose, which changes everything.

**The System's voice across the arc:**
- T-001 to T-004: mostly procedural. It is building trust through consistency, not warmth.
- T-005 to T-007: begins delivering intelligence. The voice gains context without gaining personality.
- T-008 to T-010: the single moment in T-009 where the System shows the Behavioural Trace delta is the closest the System ever comes to something like care. It should feel like an anomaly. Not sentimentality — just an unusual data selection choice that says more than it states.

**What the MC does NOT do in the first arc:**
- He does not become certain the System is supernatural or magical. He remains empirical. It works. That is his entire basis for continuing.
- He does not become a different personality. The arc is not a transformation of character but an expression of it. He was always capable. The System just gave him a direction.
- He does not find a romantic partner, a best friend, or a mentor. The world expands but he remains essentially solo through T-010. Relationships are tools and complications, not emotional resolutions.
