Adaeze's message arrived on a Tuesday.

*Something has come up on the project — the person handling the synthesis component dropped out this morning. I know this is short notice. I know I said the work would start next month. If you can do it, the deliverable is Friday. I completely understand if you can't.*

He read it at 07:54, six minutes before the morning directive.

The System messaged at 08:00 exactly.

:::system
[ INCURSION DETECTED ]

The message you have just received constitutes an INCURSION event.

Classification: Time-limited / Multi-attribute / Consequence-bearing

An Incursion is a real-world challenge that arrives unsolicited, has a defined window before it expires, and will produce a measurable consequence either way — action or inaction.

This is not a directive. You are not being instructed to accept. You are being informed of what this is so that you can decide with accurate information.

[ INCURSION PARAMETERS ]
Time window: Approximately 72 hours
Attributes required: INTELLIGENCE (primary) / ENDURANCE (secondary)
Current attribute levels: Sufficient, with effort
Consequence of acceptance: High-effort, high-output, high-exposure. You will be measured against a deliverable.
Consequence of refusal: The offer expires. The door that reopened in T-004 closes again at a different angle.

Note: The System does not issue Incursion instructions. Incursions are tests the world offers. They do not always offer them. They expire.

[ AWAITING OPERATOR DECISION ]
:::

He read the transmission twice. *They do not always offer them. They expire.*

He thought about two years ago — the call from Adaeze the first time, the offer, the polite deflection, the gradual silence that followed. He thought about the empty units on the main road and the compressed affect of the people passing them and the faction that manufactured the conditions in which refusal felt sensible.

He typed his reply to Adaeze before he typed his response to the System. *I can do it. Send me what you have.*

Then he told the System.

:::system
[ INCURSION ACCEPTED ]
[ DIRECTIVE CHAIN: ACTIVATING ]

You have 68 hours to delivery. The following directives will govern this period. Complete them in order. Do not skip ahead. Do not batch.

[ DIRECTIVE 013a — INTELLIGENCE ]
Read everything Adaeze sends you before doing anything else. Full comprehension before any output. Time estimate: 2–3 hours.

[ DIRECTIVE 013b — INTELLIGENCE / ENDURANCE ]
Produce an initial framework for the synthesis. Not the synthesis itself — the framework. The shape of the answer before the content of the answer. Time estimate: 3–4 hours.

[ DIRECTIVE 013c — AGILITY ]
Send the framework to Adaeze for alignment before proceeding. This feels premature. Do it anyway. Premature alignment prevents late-stage misdirection.

[ DIRECTIVE 013d — ENDURANCE ]
Execute the synthesis. Full output. Timer: the remaining time minus eight hours for review.

[ DIRECTIVE 013e — INTELLIGENCE / CHARISMA ]
Review, refine, deliver. The delivery is not just the document. It is the interaction. Be present for it.

[ MOMENTUM MULTIPLIER: INCURSION ACTIVE — 1.5x UNTIL COMPLETION OR EXPIRY ]
:::

---

The material Adaeze sent was dense. He read it in one sitting, two and a half hours, with the kind of concentrated attention that the INTELLIGENCE directives over the past two weeks had been building in him incrementally without his noticing the accumulation. He took notes. The notes were better than the notes he would have taken two weeks ago. He could feel this without being able to explain exactly why.

The framework came in the early afternoon — not fully formed but structurally sound, the bones of an argument that the synthesis work could build on. He sent it to Adaeze at 2pm with a note: *Before I go further — does this match how you're thinking about it, or am I at an angle?*

She replied in eleven minutes. *This is exactly right. Honestly better than what we had before. Go.*

He went.

---

The second day was the hardest.

Not because the work was beyond him — it was not, it was exactly at the edge of his current capability, which meant it required everything he had. That was the hardness. He worked past midnight and woke at six and worked again. He ate badly. He did not exercise. The System noted both.

:::system
[ MID-INCURSION STATUS CHECK ]

ENDURANCE draw: high. Expected.
INTELLIGENCE draw: high. Expected.
STRENGTH and AGILITY: temporarily deprioritised. This is acceptable for 72 hours. It is not acceptable as a sustained pattern.

You are operating within parameters.

One note: you have been working for 14 hours across the last two days and your output quality is declining at the margin. Recognised or not?
:::

He typed: *Recognised.*

:::system
[ DIRECTIVE: STOP FOR 90 MINUTES. NOW. ]

Not a suggestion. An operational requirement. Output quality below threshold costs more time than the 90-minute pause.

Sleep is not available. Food and movement are.

[ TIMER: 90 MINUTES — THEN RETURN ]
:::

He stopped. He ate a proper meal for the first time since the Incursion started. He walked around the block twice. He came back and looked at the work and saw, immediately, a structural problem in section three that he had been writing around for four hours without identifying it. He fixed it in twenty minutes.

The System did not say *I told you so.* It logged the resumed execution and updated the momentum counter.

---

He delivered the work at 10am on Friday with two hours to spare.

Adaeze's response came an hour later. He read it standing in the kitchen with cold coffee.

*This is really good work. I mean it — this is better than I expected and I expected quite a lot. I'm going to introduce you to someone. His name is Kwame. He's been building something I think you should know about. I don't want to say more until I've spoken to him, but — I think it's relevant to what you're doing.*

He read it twice. *Relevant to what you're doing.* She did not know what he was doing. Not really. She knew he was operating differently. She did not know about the System, about the Trace, about the faction briefing from T-005.

He forwarded the message to the System.

:::system
[ MESSAGE RECEIVED AND FLAGGED ]

The contact Adaeze refers to — Kwame — is not in the current operator network data. He is an unknown variable.

Note: the Incursion generated a new contact. New contacts generated by Incursions are not random. They are the consequence of demonstrating capability at sufficient level to warrant introduction.

Also note: the phrase "relevant to what you're doing" from someone who does not know what you are doing suggests she has observed something in the way you are operating that she does not have a name for yet.

[ INCURSION: COMPLETE ]
[ FINAL ATTRIBUTE UPDATES: INTELLIGENCE +1.1 / ENDURANCE +0.8 / CHARISMA +0.4 ]
[ STREAK: 19 ]
[ MOMENTUM MULTIPLIER: RETURNING TO 1.5x — INCURSION COMPLETE BONUS APPLIED ]
[ WORLD BOSS: NOT YET DESIGNATED — DESIGNATION RECOMMENDED ]

Significant data point: you delivered under time pressure at the edge of your current capability. This is the most useful kind of execution for development purposes. The System recommends seeking Incursions, not waiting for them.

And: the new contact. Kwame. The System will monitor.
:::

He sat with the final note for a moment. *The System will monitor.* It had been monitoring since before he had known it existed. He understood that now in a way he had not on the first night.

He was not sure how he felt about that. He was not sure the feeling mattered operationally.

He was tired in the specific way of having done something hard and finished it and the hardness being over. It was a good kind of tired. He had forgotten that there were good kinds.
