The offer arrived two days later, fully formed.

Not an implication, not a door held slightly open — a specific proposition with a timeline, a scope, and a rate that was higher than anything he had been offered before. The person on the other end of the message thread had done something he had not anticipated: they had not waited for him to ask for the full picture. They had assessed the 80-word reply as a sufficient signal and moved immediately to the operational question.

He read the proposition standing in the hallway with his coat still on, having just come back from the morning walk that the physical STRENGTH directive had sent him on. He read it twice. Then he stood there for a moment with the coat still on and the phone in his hand and the proposition on the screen.

He had been at step 6 of the campaign map for six days. He had not sought the Incursion as actively as the plan had implied he should — not from avoidance, from accurate timing assessment, the sense that the network effect was building and that seeking it too aggressively would push the door before it was fully open. This had been a judgment call. The System had not issued a directive on it.

The door was fully open now.

He took the coat off. He sat down. He sent the proposition to the System.

He read the chain. Six days. He had done the first Incursion in 68 hours. Six days was more space — but the scale was also larger, the expectations higher, the consequence sharper in both directions.

He made coffee. He sat down with everything they had sent and read it. All of it. Two and a half hours — slower than he would have been before the System, the INTELLIGENCE directives having built a particular quality of concentrated attention that he could now sustain for longer than was comfortable.

The thing they were asking for was more complex than the T-006 Incursion. Not impossibly complex — complexity that sat at the edge of his current capability, which the System had told him was the most developmentally productive kind. It required him to hold multiple moving parts simultaneously without collapsing them into a single frame. His tendency under pressure was to find the frame that unified everything and then build outward from it. The risk was that the frame pre-empted the complexity instead of containing it.

He noted this before he began. Named the tendency. Wrote it at the top of the notebook page:

*Do not find the frame before the work earns it.*

Then he started.

---

Day 1 and Day 2 were the reading and the map. He read everything, took notes, produced the LEVERAGE map (directive 017b) and sent it to the System. The System assessed it in six minutes.

:::system
[ LEVERAGE MAP — RECEIVED ]

Accurate. One addition: the value of this delivery is not just in the output. It is in the demonstration that you can operate at this level. The output will be assessed. How you operate across the 6 days will also be assessed — not explicitly, but through the quality of the questions you ask mid-process, the way you handle uncertainty, the professionalism of every touchpoint.

Every interaction in a Class 2 Incursion is part of the delivery.

[ 017b: COMPLETE ]
[ BEGIN 017c ]
:::

Day 3 was the hardest day of thinking he had done since starting the System. Not from emotional difficulty — from the specific challenge of holding complexity without collapsing it. He broke the problem into its component parts. He worked on each part separately. He resisted the pressure to synthesise prematurely. He felt the pressure clearly — it was the same pressure as the frame, the urge to find the unified structure before the parts had been fully understood.

He held the parts separately until late afternoon. Then he let the synthesis happen. It happened in twenty minutes.

He reported the twenty minutes to the System.

:::system
[ LOGGED ]

The synthesis was rapid because the preceding work was thorough. Premature synthesis is fast and wrong. Mature synthesis is fast and correct. The speed looks the same from outside. The distinction is in what preceded it.

[ INTELLIGENCE: +0.4 — MID-INCURSION UPDATE ]
:::

Day 4 was production. He worked from 07:00 to 22:30. He stopped twice — once for food, once because the System issued the mandatory pause directive that had also appeared in T-006.

:::system
[ MANDATORY PAUSE — 017c ]

Output quality is at 80% of optimal. This is the degradation threshold.

90 minutes. Food. Movement. No screen. Return.

[ TIMER: 90 MINUTES ]
:::

He complied without the resistance he would have had in T-006. Not because he agreed immediately — he did not, he felt the pull to push through — but because the T-006 data was in his record and he trusted it more than he trusted the feeling. The feeling was wrong in T-006. It would be wrong now.

He ate. He walked. He came back and saw a structural gap in section two that he had been building around without identifying it. He fixed it in an hour.

Day 5 was review. He read the work from the position of the person who would receive it — not from his own position, from theirs. What they needed from it, what they would notice, what they would find dense or unclear or oversimplified. He made 23 edits. Eleven were wording. Eight were structural. Four were cuts.

The cuts were the hardest. They were the four places where he had written more than the work required — where he had over-demonstrated his understanding at the cost of the reader's experience of the material. The first Incursion had the same problem in section three. He found this one in section two.

He cut cleanly. He reported.

:::system
[ REVIEW COMPLETE — ASSESSED ]

23 edits. Quality assessment: delivery-ready.

Note: you identified the over-demonstration problem in section two independently, without prompt. In T-006 the over-demonstration problem in section three was identified after the System issued the mandatory pause. The identification timing has moved earlier in the process.

AGILITY is developing in the direction the threshold event requires.

[ 017c: COMPLETE ]
[ PREPARE 017d ]
[ STREAK: 20 ]

Day 6. He prepared the delivery framing. He thought about what the System had noted: *every interaction in a Class 2 Incursion is part of the delivery.* The framing was not separate from the work. It was the final layer of the work — the context in which the work would be received, which would shape how it was understood.

He wrote the framing. He read it back. It was accurate. He sent the delivery.

Then he waited.
